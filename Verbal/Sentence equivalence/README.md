# GRE Sentence Equivalence — 500 Hard Questions

Challenging GRE-level Sentence Equivalence practice with **every option's meaning defined** and the **two correct answers marked ✅**.

## How it works

Each question is a single sentence with one blank (`______`) followed by five options. Pick the **two** choices that both fit the sentence and produce sentences alike in meaning — the two correct answers are near-synonyms in context. There is no partial credit.

**Format of each question:**

```
### 98. Generations of students came to ______ the old professor for both his learning and his kindness.

❌ A. despise = hate; regard as worthless
❌ B. disdain = look down upon; scorn
✅ C. revere = respect deeply; regard with admiration
❌ D. scorn = treat with contempt
✅ E. venerate = respect or honor deeply
```

## Tips for hard items

- Read for the sentence's logic first — especially contrast words like *although*, *despite*, *but* — then predict a word before scanning the options.
- When two synonyms both seem to fit, check whether each matches the sentence's tone and direction.
- If you find only one good answer, the second is usually a subtler synonym of it.
