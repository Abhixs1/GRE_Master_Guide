## How Reading Comprehension Works

Each passage is followed by ten questions with five choices each. GRE RC passages are dense and argumentative rather than informational: the author almost always takes a position on a debate, concedes something to the other side, and qualifies the concession. Nearly every question is really asking, *what is this author doing, and how much are they committing to?*

## Strategy

1. **Find the pivot.** Hard GRE passages turn on a *but*, *yet*, *however*, *the difficulty is*, or *the conclusion moves too fast*. The author's real claim is almost always on the far side of that pivot.
2. **Track who is speaking.** Passages ventriloquize critics, defenders, and "some scholars." A view stated in the passage is not automatically the author's view — the most common wrong answer attributes a critiqued position to the author.
3. **Prefer the weak answer.** Correct choices say *complicates*, *qualifies*, *suggests*, *partly*. Wrong choices say *proves*, *refutes entirely*, *never*, *always*. Authors of GRE passages are hedgers.
4. **Answer from the text, not the topic.** If you know the subject well, your knowledge is a liability — several distractors are true in the world but unsupported by the passage.
5. **For Strengthen/Weaken, negate the author's key assumption.** The right answer usually targets the specific bridge in the argument, not the general topic.
