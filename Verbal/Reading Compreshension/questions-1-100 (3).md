The choice between them is not merely technical; it decides whether ontology must expand to fit our language or language be explained without inflating our ontology.

## Passage 10 — Evolutionary Biology

> Adaptationist reasoning — explaining a trait by the advantage it confers — has been enormously fruitful and just as often abused. Its power lies in the discipline it imposes: a good adaptive hypothesis makes predictions that can fail. Its abuse lies in the ease with which a plausible story can be told for any feature, however incidental. A trait may persist not because it was selected but because it rode along with something that was, or because it is a by-product of developmental constraints. The corrective is not to abandon adaptive explanation but to hold it to the standard it claims for itself: to treat the adaptive story as a hypothesis to be tested, not a conclusion to be assumed.
