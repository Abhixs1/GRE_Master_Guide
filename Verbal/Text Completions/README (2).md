## How Text Completion Works

Each passage has one, two, or three blanks. One-blank items give five choices; two- and three-blank items give three choices per blank in separate columns (i), (ii), (iii). You must fill *every* blank correctly to earn credit — there is no partial credit.

## Strategy

1. **Read the whole sentence first** and pin down the logical relationship the blank serves — does the sentence reinforce an idea, reverse it (watch *although*, *yet*, *far from*, *but*), or explain it?
2. **Predict your own word**, then match it to the choices.
3. **In multi-blank items, start with the easiest blank** — the structure it reveals constrains the others.

## Format

```
### 98. Generations of students came to ______ the old professor
        for both his learning and his kindness.

❌ A. despise = hate; regard as worthless
❌ B. disdain = look down upon; scorn
✅ C. revere = respect deeply; regard with admiration
❌ D. scorn = treat with contempt
❌ E. venerate = respect or honor deeply
```
